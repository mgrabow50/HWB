Troubleshooting Guide
The Ice-E-Liminator Beam Blades are manufactured to provide long, trouble-free use. However, if you should experience any problems, please refer to the chart below for remedies.
Indication
Possible Causes
Corrective Actions
Blades not warming up

Is the power (Red) wire connected to the proper power source?

Check for 12 volts with meter.

Wiring faulty, incorrect, or grounded.

Check all wiring for proper connections.

Loose or corroded connections.

Check all terminal connections and wiring.

Is the ignition on?

Turn ignition on.

Is the Black wire properly grounded?

Check ground wire with meter.

Is the outside temperature too warm? (Above 34° F).

Sensor requires temperature to be below 34° F. May take time for sensor to activate.

Are wiper connectors connected properly?

Arrows on connectors should be lined up. Be sure ground wire is tight. Be sure fuse is firmly seated.

Is the fuse blown?

Replace fuse.
Sensor not coming on

Any place in the engine compartment can be hotter than 34° F when the engine is running.

If it works driving down the road, but not while idling, move sensor to a cooler location.

Place sensor in grille area outside of engine compartment. Place sensor as low as possible and do not place next to any heat source such as the radiator or headlights.
Blades on all the time

Power not shutting off with ignition.

Check with a volt meter that fuse placement is off when the ignition is off and has power only when the ignition is on.
Blades on in warm weather

Sensor is malfunctioning.

Disconnect sensor and check with ohmmeter. Sensor should be off when warm. (Above 46° F).

Wiring is shorted.

Check for a short to ground.

 

Some Further Tips for Proper Usage:

The blades may not feel warm to the touch, but will be warm enough to melt ice and snow.>

If the blades work going down the road, but not while idling, the sensor is likely too close to a heat source. The engine compartment warms too much for the sensor while idling. Move the sensor as low in the grille area as possible.

To test the sensor, wrap in snow for a while.>
